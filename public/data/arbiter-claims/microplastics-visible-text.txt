“Every wash leaves microplastics in the environment.” (microfibers shed during washing)

Microplastics in food and food-contact materials

“Microplastics are found in many processed foods.”
“Microplastics can get into food.” (one cited pathway in the discourse: shedding from plastic cutting boards)

Human exposure and health risk framing (often hedged, sometimes quantified)

“Microplastic accumulation in the human body could be a serious health hazard… and might be linked to cancer, heart attacks and other conditions.”
“Microplastics may pose risks to human health.”
“Only a few studies have directly examined the impact of microplastics on human health.”
“Adults ingest the equivalent of one credit card per week in microplastics.” (a widely repeated figure in public discourse; treated as a specific quantitative claim)

Pushback/minimization claim

“Microplastics are not a real problem.” (framed as “microplastics is nonsense”)

“Solutions/tech fixes” claim (mentioned but not substantiated in-post)

“A fish-inspired filter stops microplastics in washers.”
